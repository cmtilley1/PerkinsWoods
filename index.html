About Perkins

Perkins Woods is a 7.5 acre woodland remnant of what use to be apart of "The Big Woods" of Evanston. The dominance, density and frequency of Basswoods and Silver Maples recorded on site during GES 327 quarter point evaluations in 2018 indicate a Wet Forest community (Chicago Wilderness Atlas of Biodiversity, 1999.)

From 1990 to 2018



Historically, the northeast Illinois ecosystem was dominated by Oaks and Hickories.You will see that today we see that Habitat fragmentation, urban sprawl, disease, and invasive species have sped up succession in old growth forests.



Comparison Statistics

Basswood: 11.1% 1990 to 33.3% 2018 Maple: 17.2% 1990 to 29.3% 2018 Oak: 31.3% 1990 to 19.2% 2018 Hickory: 3% 1990 to 4% 2018 Ash: 34% 1990 to 2% 2018 Elm: 2% 1990 to 8% 2018


From 1990 to 2018, Basswood increased by 200%. Native Oaks decreased as invasive Norway Maples crowded the canopy. Ash decreased from 34% to 2% of the total forest composition due to Emerald Ash Borer. It can be seen that Elm trees are slowly re-establishing their population numbers. Lastly, Black cherry was not found 28 years later, but now Box Elder is present.
